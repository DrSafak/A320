9.0 Fuel System

Fuel Recirculation System

IDG Cooling

The fuel recirculation system uses fuel supplied to each engine for IDG cooling. The fuel is directed through the IDG heat exchanger to the fuel return valve, and then to the wing tank outer fuel compartments. The fuel return valve is controlled by the full authority digital engine control (FADEC). If the outer compartment is already full, the fuel that has circulated overflows to the inner compartment through a spill pipe.

If the center tank is supplying fuel, the wing tank tends to overfill; therefore, the center tank pump is automatically selected off when the inner tank is full. The wing tank supplies the engine until approximately 1100 pounds of fuel is used, and then the center tank pump resumes operation.

Some of the fuel flowing out of the hydro-mechanical unit goes to cool the oil system of the Integrated Drive Generators (IDGs). It then returns fuel to the fuel pump unit or to the tank.
