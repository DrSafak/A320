7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Control Laws

Direct Law - Description

In direct law, flight control inputs are like conventional flight controls. No protections are provided in direct law. Over speed indications, stall indications and stall aural warnings are furnished on the PFD. The PFD airspeed scale provides VMAX, VLS, and VSW. The airplane's pitch must be manually trimmed.

PITCH DIRECT - The pitch direct law is a direct side stick-to-elevator position relationship. An amber USE MAN PITCH TRIM message appears on the PFD.

ROLL DIRECT - Roll direct law is a direct side stick-to-aileron and spoiler position relationship. A maximum roll rate of 30 degrees per second can be obtained in roll direct law.

YAW MECHANICAL - Yaw is yaw mechanical and is a direct relationship with rudder movement. In yaw mechanical law, there is no turn coordination or yaw damping.

RECONFIGURATION OF FLT CTL LAWS
If multiple system failures occur, the flight control computers will reconfigure to a degraded flight control law. See Reconfiguration of FCL Diagram.
