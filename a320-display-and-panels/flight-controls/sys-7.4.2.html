7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Controls

Pitch Control

PITCH CONTROL - Pitch is controlled by the elevators and horizontal stabilizer. They are electrically controlled by the ELACs or SECs, and are hydraulically actuated.

PITCH ACTIVATION - A screw jack drives the horizontal stabilizer (THS). The screw jack is driven by two hydraulic motors that are controlled by one of three electric motors or by the mechanical trim wheel. Two electrically controlled servo-jacks drive each elevator.

FLIGHT CONTROL COMPUTERS - Normally, the elevators and stabilizer are electrically controlled by the ELACs. If both ELACs fail, pitch control is transferred to the SECs.

MECHANICAL CONTROL - The stabilizer can also be mechanically controlled (via a cable) using the pitch trim wheels, provided hydraulic power is available. Mechanical pitch trim control overrides electrical control.
