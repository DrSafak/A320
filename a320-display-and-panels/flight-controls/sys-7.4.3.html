7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Controls

Roll Control

ROLL CONTROL - Roll is controlled by the ailerons and spoilers. They are electrically controlled by the ELACs or SECs, and are hydraulically actuated.

ROLL ACTIVATION - Each aileron has two electrically controlled hydraulic servo-jacks. One of these hydraulic servo-jacks per aileron operates at a time. A servo-jack positions each spoiler. Each servo-jack receives hydraulic power from either the green, blue, or yellow hydraulic system, and controlled by SEC 1, 2, or 3.

AILERON COMPUTERS - The ailerons are normally controlled by ELAC 1. If ELAC 1 fails, control is automatically transferred to ELAC 2. In the event of dual ELAC failure roll control and LAF are provided by the spoilers only.

SPOILER COMPUTERS - Five hydraulically powered spoiler panels located on the upper surface of each wing are controlled by the three SECs. Each spoiler panel is powered by a single hydraulic system; either green, blue, or yellow.
