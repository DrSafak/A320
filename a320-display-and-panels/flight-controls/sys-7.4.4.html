7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Controls

Yaw Control

Yaw is controlled by a single rudder. The rudder is electrically controlled by trim motors, or mechanically controlled by the rudder pedals. The rudder is electrically controlled by the ELACs or FACs, and are hydraulically actuated.

RUDDER ACTIVATION - Three independent servo-jacks operate in parallel to actuate the rudder. In automatic operation (turn coordination, yaw damping) a green hydraulic actuator drives all three servo-jacks. A yellow hydraulic actuator remains synchronized, an takes over if there is a green hydraulic actuator failure.

AUTOMATIC RUDDER TRIM - In flight, with the autopilot engaged, automatic rudder trim is provided by inputs from the FACs and FMGCs. If an engine fails, FMGC and FAC signals provide rudder trim according to engine power, airspeed, bank angle, and yaw.

RUDDER LIMTER - Rudder deflection is limited as a function of airspeed. If both FACs fail, maximum rudder deflection can be obtained after the slats are extended.
 

YAW DAMPING AND TURN CORDINATION - The yaw orders for both yaw damping and turn coordination are computed by the ELACs and sent to the FACs.

MECHANICAL CONTROL - The rudder can also be mechanically controlled (via a cable) using the rudder pedals, provided hydraulic power is available.
