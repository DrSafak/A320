7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Control Laws

Normal Law - Description

Normal Law consist of three modes of operation that includes;

  • Ground Mode
  • Flight Mode, and
  • Flare Mode

The ground mode and flare mode are direct law with no active protections and no automatic trimming. The flight mode provides flight envelope protection that includes:

  • Load factor demand mode
  • Load factor limitation
  • Pitch attitude protection
  • High speed protection
  • High AOA protection (Alpha Prot, Alpha Floor, and Alpha Max)
  • Bank angle protection, and
  • Load Alleviation Function (on earlier aircraft)

YAW NORMAL LAW - Yaw in normal law uses the rudder for turn coordination and yaw damping.

The rudder pedals directly command rudder surface movement via cables.

RECONFIGURATION OF FLT CTL LAWS
If multiple system failures occur, the flight control computers will reconfigure to a degraded flight control law. See Reconfiguration of FCL Diagram.
