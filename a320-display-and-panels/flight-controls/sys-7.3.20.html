7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Control Laws

Abnormal Attitude Law - Description

The abnormal attitude law activates when any one of the following parameters is exceeded:

  • Pitch attitude is greater than 50° nose-up or 30° nose-down
  • Angle of attack is greater than 30° or less than -10° (A319: -15°)
  • Airspeed is greater than 440 knots or less than 60 knots or Mach is greater than .91 or less than .1
  • Bank angle is greater than 125°

When the abnormal attitude law is activated, the following laws are incurred until the airplane returns to normal limits:

  • Pitch alternate with load factor protection and without automatic trim nor any other protections
  • Roll direct
  • Yaw mechanical

RECONFIGURATION OF FLT CTL LAWS
If multiple system failures occur, the flight control computers will reconfigure to a degraded flight control law. See Reconfiguration of FCL Diagram.
