7.0 Flight Controls

Flight Control Laws

Normal Law - Bank Angle Protection

In normal law the roll attitude is held constant with the side stick at neutral (up to 33° of bank). If full stick deflection is held, the bank angle is limited to a maximum of 67°. If the side stick is released to neutral with a bank angle greater than 33°, the bank angle decreases automatically to approximately 33°. Above 33°, the automatic pitch trim is inoperative.

If high angle of attack protection is active, the bank angle is limited to 45°. If high speed protection is active, the bank angle is limited to 45° and a 0° bank angle is commanded with the side stick neutral.

If the bank angle exceeds 45°, the autopilot automatically disconnects and the flight director bars disappear.
